# Embracing AI: Why Using Technology to Enhance Our Capabilities is Absolutely Acceptable

![Embracing AI: Why Using Technology to Enhance Our Capabilities is Absolutely Acceptable](/assets/2025/02/embracing-ai-why-using-technology-to-enhance-our-capabilities-is-absolutely-acceptable-i-resent-it.png)


We’ve all heard it before: “If you’re using AI, aren’t you cheating a little?” It’s a sentiment that, admittedly, might give us pause. After all, less than a century ago, people faced similar scrutiny for undergoing cosmetic surgery, a mere few decades ago for choosing a calculator over mental math, and even now (still?) for opting to drive instead of walking. Societal judgment has always greeted new innovations – right before embracing them as normal.

Today, we find ourselves at the same juncture with AI. The truth is, we often romanticize “doing it the hard way,” but in many cases, that isn’t practical or even the best way to achieve results. Just as we don’t expect accountants to calculate every figure by hand or surgeons to reject modern medical advancements, we shouldn’t feel reluctant to let AI help us work more efficiently.

Consider the history of the calculator. When it first hit the scene, many thought it undermined our mathematical integrity. Teachers, parents, and mathematicians alike worried that using a calculator would make us lazy thinkers. But as calculators grew commonplace, our collective attitude shifted. They were eventually seen as tools to free up mental bandwidth for more complex thinking and creativity.

Similarly, early adopters of cosmetic surgery were often judged, yet today, it’s seen mainly as a personal choice for self-improvement, one that society accepts and even encourages. The same shift is happening with AI. The knee-jerk fear that “AI does the work for you” misses a key point: AI doesn’t take away the work; it enhances our ability to deliver, create, and succeed. Whether drafting an article, solving a complex problem, or crunching data, AI has become a tool to amplify what we do best, not replace it.

And yes, this article? It was “enhanced” in part by various AI tools. That doesn’t mean it lacks human thought or creativity; it means I collaborated with technology to craft a message that was more efficient, polished, and resonant than if I’d done it entirely solo. By welcoming AI as a partner, we unlock new potential and free up time to focus on what truly requires our unique human touch.

So, the next time someone questions the ethics or authenticity of using AI, remember that societal judgment has faded over time with every new technology we’ve accepted as useful and beneficial. One day soon, using AI will be as normal as grabbing a calculator or hopping in a car. And we’ll wonder why we ever hesitated.

What do you think, is using AI cheating?

# Alchemy to AI: Historical Parallels in Public and Governmental Attitudes Towards Groundbreaking Technologies

![Alchemy to AI: Historical Parallels in Public and Governmental Attitudes Towards Groundbreaking Technologies](/assets/2024/04/alchemy-to-ai-historical-parallels-in-public-and-governmental-attitudes-towards-groundbreaking-technologies-alchemy-to-ai.png)


In the medieval period, the practice of alchemy captivated the imagination and fear of both the public and the government. Alchemists, driven by the pursuit to transmute base metals into gold, were seen not only as pioneers of early chemical science but also as potential threats to the economic and political stability of the era. This dual perception led to the enactment of the “Act Against Multiplication” by King Henry IV of England in 1404, a law that outlawed the creation of gold and silver, a core goal of alchemy. The concerns were primarily economic: the ability to “multiply” gold could destabilize currency value and diminish governmental control over wealth.

Fast forward to the 21st century, and we find ourselves in a similar quandary with the advent of artificial intelligence (AI). AI, like alchemy, stands at the crossroads of immense potential and profound societal concern. As AI technologies rapidly advance, they promise revolutionary changes in myriad sectors, including healthcare, transportation, and finance. However, they also pose significant challenges, such as job displacement, privacy issues, and potential misuse.

**Economic and Political Power Shifts**

Just as Henry IV feared the economic implications of alchemy, modern governments are grappling with the economic shifts driven by AI. Automation threatens to disrupt traditional job markets, and the creation of vast amounts of wealth is increasingly concentrated in the hands of those who control the most advanced AI technologies. Governments worldwide are thus in a race to establish regulations that balance innovation with economic stability and job security.

**Public Perception and Ethical Concerns**

In medieval times, alchemy was shrouded in mystery and suspicion, often associated with magical or supernatural elements. Today, AI often elicits a similar mixed sense of wonder and unease among the public. The ethical implications of AI, from facial recognition surveillance to decision-making in autonomous vehicles, evoke significant debate on the limits and controls of these technologies.

**Regulation and Control**

The “Act Against Multiplication” can be seen as a historical precursor to contemporary calls for stricter AI regulations. Just as Boyle eventually advocated for repealing the alchemy ban, promoting the study of chemistry as a legitimate scientific endeavour, modern scientists and technologists argue for nuanced AI governance that encourages innovation while safeguarding ethical standards and public welfare.

**Advancements in Scientific Understanding**

The transition from alchemy to chemistry during the Renaissance period highlighted a maturation in scientific understanding, much as current advancements in AI are expanding our comprehension of both technology and its potential human impact. Early scientists like Boyle and Newton, who dabbled in alchemy, laid down the groundwork for modern scientific disciplines. Similarly, today’s AI researchers are setting the stage for future technological and ethical frameworks that may well define the next era of human civilization.

**Conclusion**

The historical narrative of alchemy offers a fascinating lens through which to view contemporary issues surrounding AI. Both fields represent the cutting edge of their respective times’ technological and scientific understanding and have provoked significant public and governmental reactions. By studying past responses to disruptive technologies, modern policymakers and technologists can better navigate the ethical, social, and economic implications of AI, ensuring that its development benefits society as a whole. This approach not only acknowledges the transformative potential of AI but also respects the profound responsibilities that accompany its use.
